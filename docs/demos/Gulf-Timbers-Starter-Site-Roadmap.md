Gulf Timbers Starter Site Roadmap
The Big Idea
Gulf Timbers is a fictional coastal town used to showcase SystemStrap starter sites.

Instead of building disconnected demo layouts, each starter site belongs to the same shared town. Every business, organization, landmark, and event helps make the theme feel more alive.

The goal is not just to show what SystemStrap can do.

The goal is to make people remember it.

Core Concept
SystemStrap starter sites are not generic demos.

They are fictional, story-driven, one-page site concepts that can later grow into full child themes.

Each starter site should feel like a real local business or community organization with:

a name
a story
a visual identity
a memorable setting
reusable images
layout sections
connections to the rest of Gulf Timbers
Gulf Timbers
Gulf Timbers is a small coastal town just a stone’s throw from the best saltwater fishing this side of the Feather Ridge Range.

It has:

a historic Main Street
a town square
Gulf Coast marshland
old oak trees
local shops
family businesses
a wildlife refuge
a city hall
a creek
nearby fishing spots
a community that knows each other
The town itself becomes the shared background for every starter site.

Why This Matters
Most theme demos are isolated.

A restaurant demo has nothing to do with the medical demo. The shop demo has nothing to do with the city demo.

Gulf Timbers changes that.

A butcher shop can sponsor the BBQ festival.
The hearing center can sponsor the softball team.
The nursery can provide flowers for City Hall.
The wildlife refuge can host school field trips.
The coffee shop can serve everyone on Main Street.

The demos start to feel connected.

Starter Sites
Planned starter site concepts include:

Cliff’s Cuts
A family butcher and grocery shop with a 47-year legacy, famous rub, local products, and WooCommerce-style product layouts.

Gulf Timbers Hearing Center
A small audiology practice led by Dr. Evelyn Sloan, supported by Beth at the front desk, focused on elderly patients, community care, volunteer outreach, and local softball.

Greenleaf Nursery
A garden center with greenhouses, indoor and outdoor plants, seasonal products, landscaping supplies, and community roots.

Gulf Timbers Wildlife Refuge
A wildlife refuge or small zoo featuring a massive alligator, native birds, marsh trails, conservation programs, education, memberships, and events.

Gulf Timbers City Hall
A municipal starter site for departments, notices, forms, meetings, permits, events, and public services.

Future ideas may include a coffee shop, pizza place, marina, bait shop, orchard, farm supply store, veterinary clinic, campground, and local newspaper.

The One-Page Rule
For now, each starter site begins as a single-page layout.

That page should demonstrate:

hero section
story/about section
service or product cards
image panels
CTA sections
team or people section
FAQ or accordion
footer/contact section
The one-page version is the proof of concept.

Later, each one can become a full child theme.

Story First
Every starter site begins with a written story.

The story should feel like a feature article about a real local business.

Not corporate filler.

Not lorem ipsum.

Not generic marketing.

The story should explain:

who runs the business
why it exists
what makes it memorable
how it fits into Gulf Timbers
what kind of layouts and imagery it needs
Asset Planning
Each starter site should include an image inventory.

For every image, define:

filename
dimensions
purpose
prompt
placement in the layout
Images should be generated or prepared at final production sizes so patterns can reference stable paths directly.

Layout Planning
Each starter site should include a layout outline.

Example:

Hero
About / origin story
Services or products
Featured callout
Gallery or carousel
Community section
CTA
Contact/footer
The layout should be designed to show off SystemStrap’s strengths without feeling like a component catalog.

Recipes Tab
The final demo area can include a Recipes tab.

Recipes explain how selected layouts were built.

A recipe can show:

which core blocks were used
which SystemStrap variations were applied
why the layout works
what image sizes are used
how sections stack responsively
This turns the demos into learning material without making the starter sites feel technical.

Child Theme Future
Eventually, each Gulf Timbers starter site can become its own child theme.

The child theme would include:

its own colors
starter patterns
images
demo content
typography choices
one-page or multi-page layout
optional WooCommerce support where appropriate
The shared SystemStrap parent theme provides the structure.
The child theme provides the personality.

Guiding Principle
Nothing should exist only because it is a demo.

If there is a pricing table, it should belong to a real concept.
If there is a carousel, it should show real products or projects.
If there is an accordion, it should answer real questions.
If there is a CTA, it should make sense for that business.

Every layout should feel useful inside the fictional world.

The Goal
SystemStrap should not ship with forgettable demo content.

It should ship with a place.

A town.

A set of businesses and stories people remember.

Gulf Timbers becomes the creative home for SystemStrap starter sites.

You’re right. The media system should reflect **one town with many visual cultures**, not one aesthetic repeated everywhere.

The unifying thread should be photographic realism, consistent geography, believable people, and recurring locations—not identical color grading or mood.

# Revised Gulf Timbers Image Board Plan

## Board 1 — Town Identity and Shared Geography

```text

02. Historic Main Street
03. Town square and gazebo
04. City Hall exterior
05. Marina at sunrise
06. Feather Ridge landscape
07. Gulf Coast marsh
08. Wildlife refuge boardwalk
09. Covered bridge
10. Residential street
11. Water tower

```

### Visual direction

- Highly realistic documentary photography
- Natural daylight
- Moderate saturation
- No painterly skies or exaggerated glow
- Broad, neutral compositions that work in every variation

This board supplies the universal images for the parent theme and primary screenshot.

# The Photographic Standard

Every final board should follow these rules:

- Photorealistic, not painterly
- Natural skin and material texture
- No exaggerated golden haze
- No impossible architecture
- No fake depth-of-field everywhere
- No visible commercial trademarks
- No rounded crops
- No baked-in borders
- Minimal text in generated imagery
- Consistent Gulf Timbers geography
- Business-specific styling without losing town continuity

## Three visual families

The complete library should deliberately support three broad moods:

### Heritage and Community

Cliff’s Cuts, Main Street, Book Nook, farmers market, local events.

### Civic and Professional

City Hall, bank, hearing center, corporate outlet, public services.

### Nature and Recreation

Wildlife refuge, marina, hotel, golf, zip line, trails.

Those three families cover the entire range of SystemStrap’s variations much better than forcing everything into one nostalgic editorial style.

The revised boards should demonstrate that Gulf Timbers is a believable town where **rustic, formal, playful, corporate, civic, natural, and contemporary experiences all coexist**.
